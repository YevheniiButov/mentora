Chapter 4 discusses the importance of forensic dentistry in legal contexts, emphasizing the need for proper protocols to ensure bitemark evidence is admissible in court. It outlines the procedures for collecting photographic evidence, study casts, and DNA from salivary traces. The chapter also introduces terms to describe the confidence level in identifying a bitemark and provides classifications for associating a suspect with a bitemark. Additionally, it explores comparative dental anatomy, highlighting the evolution of tooth forms across different species, from simple single cones to complex quadritubercular molars, and discusses the relationship between tooth form and jaw function in various animals, including humans.
